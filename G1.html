Logarithmic Relationships for Druid

This page explores the logarithmic relationships between key properties of a collapsing star and the reducing radius, ultimately forming a "Druid" particle. The following logarithmic properties are analyzed:

  • Density (log): As the radius decreases, the density increases logarithmically, reflecting the extreme compression of matter into a smaller volume.
  • Entropy (log): The entropy also increases logarithmically, signifying the rising disorder and energy dissipation during the collapse.
  • Force (log): The gravitational force at the surface grows logarithmically as the radius shrinks, due to the increasing mass concentration.

Key Observations

The logarithmic trends highlight the following phenomena:

  • The density of the star approaches near-infinite values as it collapses into a "Druid" particle, corresponding to an extremely high mass concentration in a minuscule radius.
  • Entropy increases exponentially, representing the irreversible transformation of ordered energy states into disordered forms.
  • The gravitational force at the surface grows dramatically as the radius approaches the Planck scale, showcasing the intense gravitational effects of the collapsing star.
