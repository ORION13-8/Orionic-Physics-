The Collapse of a Star: Gravitational Binding Energy, Density, and Probabilities

The graph illustrates several critical concepts in astrophysics concerning stellar collapse:

  • Gravitational Binding Energy: Represented in red, it shows the energy required to overcome gravitational forces holding the star together.
  • Schwarzschild Radius (R_s): The blue dashed line represents the Schwarzschild radius, the critical radius at which a star collapses into a black hole.
  • Density: Shown in green, density decreases logarithmically with the increase in radius, highlighting the inverse relationship between a star's size and its density during collapse.
  • Black Hole Formation Probability: The orange shaded region indicates the likelihood of black hole formation as the star collapses.
  • Universe Creation Probability: The purple shaded region represents the theoretical probability of creating a new universe during extreme collapse scenarios, which remains extremely small.

This graph combines theoretical physics and cosmology to show how extreme gravitational conditions can lead to black hole formation and explore the fascinating, though highly speculative, concept of universe creation.
